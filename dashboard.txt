================================================================================
                    PRODUCTIONFLOW CRM - DASHBOARD GUIDE
================================================================================

This document explains what everything on your main Dashboard represents, written
in simple words. The dashboard is divided into four main parts: KPI Cards, Charts,
Calendar, and Module Summaries. It also reflects the latest Service Steps event date
handling across Pre-Production, On-Production, and Post-Production.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. TOP ROW KPI CARDS (Core Business Numbers)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Total Revenue:
  - What it is: The total amount of money your company has earned.
  - How it is calculated: It adds up the total money from these sources:
    1) Monthly Financial Reports
    2) Clients Editing Work
    3) Camera Rentals

* Total Expenses:
  - What it is: The total amount of money your company spent.
  - How it is calculated: It adds up:
    1) Regular company expenses
    2) Freelancer fees paid
    3) Investments made to grow the company

* Net Profit:
  - What it is: The actual money you made after paying all expenses.
  - How it is calculated: Total Revenue minus Total Expenses.
  - Color Indicator: Green means you are in profit; Red means you are in loss.

* Lead Conversion Rate:
  - What it is: The percentage of leads (inquiries) that became actual clients.
  - How it is calculated: (Confirmed Leads / Total Inquiries) x 100.
  - Subtext: Displays the exact count of confirmed leads out of the total.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. SECOND ROW KPI CARDS (Secondary Details)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Clients & Pipeline:
  - What it is: Displays total client count and the status of your projects.
  - Details:
    1) Total distinct clients across all tables
    2) Total projects registered
    3) Ongoing projects (currently active)
    4) Completed projects (finished work)

* Receivables & Collections:
  - What it is: The money still pending from your clients.
  - Details: Shows the exact pending amount in numbers and words, plus the count
    of projects that need payment recovery.

* Outflows Summary:
  - What it is: Breakdown of your spending.
  - Details: Shows the money spent on growing the company (investments) and the
    money paid to freelancers.

* Sub-Service Revenue:
  - What it is: Revenue from specific side services.
  - Details: Shows the money earned from renting cameras and from editing projects.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. VISUAL ANALYTICS (Charts & Progress Bars)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Monthly Business Growth & Financial Trends:
  - Chart type: Line chart.
  - What it shows: Month-by-month changes in Revenue, Expenses, and Profit.
    It helps you see whether business performance is improving or slowing down.

* Lead Generation & Conversion Rate:
  - Chart type: Donut chart.
  - What it shows: Ratio of Confirmed (green), Pending (orange), and Rejected (red)
    leads.

* Work Completion & Service Progress:
  - Bar type: Horizontal progress bars.
  - What it shows: The average completion percentage for all clients in:
    1) Pre-Production
    2) On-Production
    3) Post-Production
    4) Checklists

* Lead Generation Channels (Platform Sources):
  - Chart type: Bar chart.
  - What it shows: Where your leads are coming from (JD, Meta Ads, Word of Mouth).

* Project Status Distribution:
  - Chart type: Polar area chart.
  - What it shows: Comparison of how many projects are in Pre-Production,
    On-Production, and Post-Production.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. UPCOMING EVENTS CALENDAR
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* What it is: A calendar list on the side of the dashboard.
* What it shows: Your next 10 upcoming shoots in chronological order.
  It shows the date, the client name, the event type, and whether the shoot is
  Confirmed or Pending.
* Event Day Display: When an event has multiple selected dates, the dashboard shows
  only the day numbers as a comma-separated list. Example: "26, 27, 28".
* Why it matters: This keeps the calendar compact while still showing multiple
  event days clearly for each client record.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. MODULE SUMMARIES (Recent Items Lists)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
At the bottom, there are 6 sections showing the 5 most recent records for:
* Pre-Production: Client names, selected event day numbers, and how much of their
  prep work is completed.
* On-Production: Client names, selected event day numbers, and on-shoot task
  completion status.
* Post-Production: Client names, selected event day numbers, deadlines, and whether
  the project is Overdue or Closed.
* Checklist Completion: Client names, whether equipment is ready, and checklist status.
* Recent Financial Transactions: Clients' total bill amount and whether they paid by
  Cash or Online.
* Client Follow-ups & Leads: Latest inquiries, platforms, and their current lead status.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. SERVICE STEPS EVENT DATE UPDATE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This dashboard now reflects the updated Event Date behavior across the following modules:
* Pre-Production
* On-Production
* Post-Production

What changed in these modules:
- Users can select multiple event dates when creating or editing records.
- The system converts selected dates into day numbers before saving.
- The database stores event dates as a string of comma-separated day numbers,
  such as "26, 27, 28".
- Dashboard tables, reports, and detail views show the same comma-separated day
  number format.
- Event date fields were updated to support VARCHAR(255) so multiple days can be stored.

This makes event scheduling easier and more consistent across the dashboard,
reports, and service steps modules.
